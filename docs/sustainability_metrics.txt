# Sustainability Metrics & ESG Scoring

## 1. ESG Framework Overview

ESG = Environmental, Social, Governance assessment of sustainability

### Oil Palm Industry Context
- Global production: 70 million metric tons/year
- Land use: ~20 million hectares
- Key sustainability concerns:
  - Deforestation and habitat loss
  - Carbon emissions vs. climate goals
  - Labor practices and land rights
  - Certification and traceability

---

## 2. Environmental (E) - 40% Weight

### 2.1 Carbon Metrics

#### Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Emissions Calculation
```
Scope 1 (Direct): On-farm equipment, fertilizer application, land use change
- Diesel generators: 2.35 kg CO2/liter
- Fertilizer production: 2.5-3.0 kg CO2/kg N, 0.5 kg CO2/kg K
- Pesticide application: 1.5 kg CO2/liter sprayed

Scope 2 (Indirect): Purchased electricity, outsourced mill
- Grid electricity: 0.4-1.2 kg CO2/kWh (varies by region)
- Mill processing: 0.5-2 kg CO2/kg CPO

Scope 3 (Value chain): Transport, end-of-life
- Logistics to mill: 50-200 kg CO2/ton CPO
- International shipping: $50-200/ton, carbon footprint
```

#### Carbon Sequestration Benefit
```
Oil Palm Carbon Storage:
- Aboveground biomass: 80-120 tons CO2/hectare accumulated over 25 years
- Annual sequestration rate: 3.2-4.8 tons CO2/hectare/year
- Soil carbon maintenance: 20-40 tons CO2/hectare (from soil management)
- Total net benefit: 2-4 tons CO2/hectare/year (after emissions subtracted)

Biomass Utilization Benefit:
- Using 1 kg biomass replaces 1 kg fossil fuel = 2.3 kg CO2 avoided
- 4,500 kg EFB/palm = 10.35 tons CO2 avoided by biomass utilization
- PKS: 2.76 tons CO2 offset annually
- Total biomass offset: 13 tons CO2/palm/year (for 1,000 palm plantation)
```

#### Carbon Footprint Calculation (Per kg CPO)
```
Typical calculation (Indonesia/Malaysia):
- Land use change: 0-4 kg CO2/kg CPO (highest if recent forest conversion)
- Cultivation: 0.8-1.2 kg CO2/kg CPO
- Processing (crushing): 0.5-1.0 kg CO2/kg CPO
- Transport to port: 0.2-0.4 kg CO2/kg CPO
- International shipping: 0.1-0.2 kg CO2/kg CPO
- TOTAL: 2.0-6.8 kg CO2/kg CPO

Best practice (biomass energy): 2-3 kg CO2/kg CPO
Conventional (fossil inputs): 4-7 kg CO2/kg CPO
```

#### ESG Environmental Score Component
```
Carbon Intensity Score (0-40 points):
- <3 kg CO2/kg CPO = 40 points (excellent)
- 3-4 kg CO2/kg CPO = 30 points (good)
- 4-5 kg CO2/kg CPO = 20 points (average)
- >5 kg CO2/kg CPO = 10 points (poor)

Biomass Utilization Bonus: +5 points if >80% biomass utilized
Carbon Offset Programs: +3 points if certified (RSPO, VCS)
```

### 2.2 Water Management

#### Water Consumption & Quality
```
Water Requirements:
- Oil palm irrigation: 1,500-2,500 mm/year
- Processing water: 5-10 tons water per ton FFB
- Total: ~8 tons water per 1 ton CPO

Water Quality Standards:
- Effluent discharge: <100 mg/L BOD, <50 mg/L total P
- pH range: 6.5-8.5
- No pesticides in surface water (detection limit approach)
```

#### Water Stewardship Score
```
Components (0-25 points):
- Water recycling: 40-60% recycling = 10 points
- Wastewater treatment: 80%+ removal efficiency = 8 points
- Riparian buffer zones: 30m minimum = 5 points
- Rainwater harvesting: >50% captured for secondary use = 2 points
```

### 2.3 Biodiversity & Land Use

#### Biodiversity Metrics
```
High Conservation Value (HCV) Concept:
1. Threatened/endangered species concentration
2. Large landscape-level ecosystems
3. Rare, threatened, or endemic ecosystems
4. Critical ecosystem services
5. Community cultural values
6. Indigenous/community significance

HCV Assessment:
- Reserve 10-30% of concession as protection
- Wildlife corridors maintained (minimum 100m width)
- No conversion of HCV areas

Biodiversity Score (0-15 points):
- >20% land reserved for conservation = 15 points
- 10-20% reserved = 10 points
- <10% reserved = 5 points
- Active restoration program = +3 points
```

#### Soil Health
```
Soil Health Indicators:
- Organic matter content: 3-5% optimal (vs. <1% degraded)
- Microbial biomass: >500 μg C/g soil = healthy
- Earthworm density: >200/m² indicates good quality
- Infiltration rate: >5 mm/hr acceptable
- pH stability: 5.5-7.0 optimal

Soil Management Score (0-10 points):
- Cover cropping + mulching = 10 points
- Minimal tillage, organic inputs = 8 points
- Conventional management = 5 points
- Erosion visible, compacted soil = 2 points
```

## 3. Social (S) - 35% Weight

### 3.1 Labor Practices
```
Fair Labor Standards:
- Wages: Minimum wage compliance + 15% premium for skilled work
- Working hours: <48 hours/week, 1 day rest per week
- Child labor: ZERO tolerance (ILO conventions)
- Occupational safety: <5 incidents per 1,000 workers/year
- Training: 40+ hours/year per worker

Labor Score (0-15 points):
- All standards met + benefits program = 15 points
- Minor gaps in training/hours = 10 points
- Wage below minimum or safety issues = 5 points
- Child labor detected = 0 points
```

### 3.2 Community Relations
```
Stakeholder Engagement:
- Community consultation: Quarterly meetings minimum
- Land rights: Documented, disputes <5% of concession
- Local employment: 40%+ workforce from surrounding communities
- Community development: 2-5% of profit to education/health
- Grievance mechanism: Documented, resolved within 60 days
- Transparency: Public disclosure of operations, wages, certifications

Community Relations Score (0-12 points):
- Active engagement + high local employment = 12 points
- Regular meetings, land rights secure = 9 points
- Limited engagement or disputes = 5 points
- Significant conflicts = 0 points
```

### 3.3 Indigenous Rights
```
Free, Prior & Informed Consent (FPIC):
- Consultation before any project initiation
- Decision-making power for affected communities
- Documented agreements maintained
- ZERO conversion of indigenous lands without consent

Indigenous Rights Score (0-8 points):
- Full FPIC, no land conflicts = 8 points
- Documented agreements, minor issues = 5 points
- Disputes or inadequate consultation = 2 points
```

## 4. Governance (G) - 25% Weight

### 4.1 Environmental Compliance
```
Regulatory Compliance:
- Environmental permits: Current, all certifications valid
- Waste management: Hazardous waste tracked, proper disposal
- Emissions monitoring: Air quality standards met
- Audit frequency: Annual minimum, public reporting

Compliance Score (0-10 points):
- Full compliance, proactive management = 10 points
- Minor infractions, quickly resolved = 7 points
- Multiple warnings or non-compliance = 3 points
- Legal action pending = 0 points
```

### 4.2 Supply Chain Transparency
```
Traceability Requirements:
- Lot tracking: From field to mill to export documented
- Digital records: Blockchain or certified system (RSPO, ISCC)
- Third-party audit: Independent verification annually
- Documentation: >90% of product traceable to origin field

Transparency Score (0-8 points):
- Full blockchain traceability = 8 points
- Digital records, annual audit = 6 points
- Paper records, limited traceability = 3 points
- No documented traceability = 0 points
```

### 4.3 Certification & External Standards
```
Relevant Certifications:
- RSPO (Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil): Highest standard
- ISCC (International Sustainability & Carbon Certification)
- MSPO (Malaysian Sustainable Palm Oil)
- ISPO (Indonesian Sustainable Palm Oil)

Certification Score (0-7 points):
- RSPO certified = 7 points
- ISCC certified = 6 points
- National standard (MSPO/ISPO) = 4 points
- No certification = 0 points
```

## 5. Integrated ESG Score Calculation

### Total Score Calculation (0-100)
```
ESG Total = (E × 0.40) + (S × 0.35) + (G × 0.25)

Where:
E (Environmental, max 40):
  - Carbon emissions (0-40)
  - Bonus: Biomass utilization (+5)
  - Penalty: Deforestation (-10 to 0)

S (Social, max 35):
  - Labor practices (0-15)
  - Community relations (0-12)
  - Indigenous rights (0-8)

G (Governance, max 25):
  - Compliance (0-10)
  - Transparency (0-8)
  - Certification (0-7)
```

### Rating Categories
```
Score | Rating | Description
-----|--------|---------------------------------------------------
85-100| AAA    | Excellent: Best-in-class sustainability
75-84 | AA     | Good: Above average, meets all standards
60-74 | A      | Average: Meets basic standards
45-59 | B      | Below Average: Multiple improvement areas
<45   | C      | Poor: Significant concerns, intervention needed
```

## 7. ESG Improvement Roadmap (Generic Template)

### Year 1 (Quick Wins)
- Implement biomass utilization program: +5 ESG points
- Conduct FPIC consultation with communities: +3 ESG points
- Establish digital traceability: +2 ESG points
- **Target ESG**: 55-65 (from baseline 40-50)

### Year 2 (Medium-term)
- Apply for MSPO/ISPO certification: +4 ESG points
- Establish community development fund (2% of profit): +3 ESG points
- Soil health improvement program (cover crops, mulch): +2 ESG points
- **Target ESG**: 65-75

### Year 3-5 (Long-term Excellence)
- Transition to RSPO certification: +5 ESG points
- Carbon offset programs (biochar, reforestation): +3 ESG points
- Advanced water treatment, zero discharge: +2 ESG points
- **Target ESG**: 75-85+ (AAA rating achievable)

## 8. Financial Benefits of ESG

### Market Premiums for Certified CPO
```
Standard CPO: $550-650/ton (market price)
MSPO certified: +$30-50/ton (5-8% premium)
ISCC certified: +$40-80/ton (7-12% premium)
RSPO certified: +$75-150/ton (12-20% premium)

Example - 1,000 ton plantation output:
- Standard: $600,000/year
- RSPO: $720,000/year (20% increase)
- Certification cost: $15,000-25,000/year
- Net benefit: $695,000-705,000/year
```

### ESG Financing Access
```
Green financing rates (2-3% lower than standard):
- Sustainable agriculture loans: 4-6% vs. 7-9% conventional
- Climate bonds: 2-4% interest (climate projects)
- Development bank financing: 3-5% preferential rates

Example loan impact:
- $1M plantation loan at 3% (ESG financing) vs. 8% (conventional)
- Annual savings: $50,000 (year 1)
- Cumulative over 10 years: $277,500
```

### Risk Mitigation
```
ESG reduces:
- Regulatory compliance costs: 10-20% reduction
- Supply chain disruption: 30% lower probability
- Reputational damage: Insurable, premium reduces 5-10%
- Market access loss: Maintains premium market access
```
